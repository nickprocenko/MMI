# Petroleum Equipment Mechanic (PM.1 / PM.2 / PM.3)

**Authority:** TSSA — Technical Standards and Safety Authority (Ontario)
**Provider:** OPCA (Ontario Petroleum Contractors Association) or fuelssafety.ca
**Mandatory Prerequisite:** PMH certificate (no exceptions)

This folder covers the full Petroleum Mechanic (PM) certification pathway. There are three levels — you pursue them in order based on the work you want to do.

---

## PM Levels — What Each Authorizes

| Level | Authorizes | Hours Required |
|---|---|---|
| **PM.1** | Service & maintain dispensers, suction pumps, submersible pumps and related systems | 40 hrs |
| **PM.2** | Install, remove, alter, test, service, and maintain **underground** installations (tanks, piping) | 40 hrs |
| **PM.3** | Install, remove, alter, test, service, and maintain **aboveground** installations | 40 hrs |

> Start with **PM.1** — it covers the most common day-to-day service work (dispensers and pumps). PM.2 and PM.3 expand into tank and piping installation which significantly grows the scope of billable work.

## Enrollment (PM.1 — start here)

- [ ] PMH certificate obtained (mandatory gate)
- [ ] Register for PM.1 course via OPCA or fuelssafety.ca
- [ ] Receive course materials / access

## Certification Steps — PM.1

- [ ] Complete PM.1 training course (40 hours)
- [ ] Pass TSSA written exam (75% pass mark)
- [ ] Log required practical field experience
- [ ] Apply for and receive PM.1 Certificate of Qualification from TSSA

## Certification Steps — PM.2 (after PM.1)

- [ ] Complete PM.2 training course (40 hours)
- [ ] Pass TSSA written exam
- [ ] Log required practical field experience (within 2 years of course completion)
- [ ] Apply for and receive PM.2 Certificate of Qualification from TSSA

## Certification Steps — PM.3 (after PM.1)

- [ ] Complete PM.3 training course (40 hours)
- [ ] Pass TSSA written exam
- [ ] Log required practical field experience
- [ ] Apply for and receive PM.3 Certificate of Qualification from TSSA
