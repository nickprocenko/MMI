# Technicians

Recognized technicians employed by a Measurement Canada registered organization are authorized to examine measuring devices and issue examination certificates for the device types listed on the organization's Schedule A.

Each course folder below contains a modular README with checklists for enrollment, certification steps, documents, and business milestones.

---

## Recommended Learning Path

> **Strategy:** Measurement Canada certs authorize you to **inspect and certify** meters. TSSA certs authorize you to **install and service** equipment. Together, MMI can offer fuel retailers a single call for everything — a much stronger business than inspections alone.

```
STEP 1 — START NOW (online, self-paced)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Liquid Fuels Handling Code 2017    $175 + HST
    → Ontario's legal foundation; also helps prep for MC practical

STEP 2 — SHORT TERM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Petroleum Mechanic Helper (PMH)    $449 + HST ($507.37 total)
    → Mandatory gate to PM.1 / PM.2 / PM.3
    → Can replace nozzles, hoses, filters on dispensers independently

STEP 3 — WHILE WAITING FOR MC PRACTICAL (before April 2027)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Site Operator — Retail             $329 + HST
    → Operate retail fuel dispensing sites (gas stations)
  PM.1 (via OPCA or fuelssafety.ca)
    → Service & maintain dispensers and submersible pumps

STEP 4 — APRIL 2027
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  MC Practical Evaluation            (scheduled with Measurement Canada)
    → Authorized to certify retail dispensers and bulk plant meters

STEP 5 — AFTER PRACTICAL
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  PM.2  → Install/remove underground tanks and piping
  PM.3  → Install/remove aboveground installations
  Vehicle Tank Meters (NTTP) → Certify fuel delivery truck meters
  NAWDS (NTTP)               → Certify commercial scales
```
